## Draft

Heads up if you delivered for Just Eat, Uber Eats, Deliveroo, Stuart, or Amazon Flex any time in 2025.

The HMRC Digital Platform Reporting rules went live on 1 January 2024. The first batch of reports were due to HMRC by 31 January 2026, covering the 2025 calendar year. That means HMRC now has your earnings figures from every gig platform you worked on, reported directly by the platforms.

Two things worth knowing:

1. If you've been declaring everything on Self Assessment, you're fine. The reports just back up what you already filed.

2. If you haven't been declaring, the figures are now visible to HMRC. The window for quietly catching up is closing.

The flip side, which most drivers miss: every business mile you can prove is a deduction the platform did not subtract. Mileage is the largest single allowable expense for most couriers.

- Cars: 45p per mile for the first 10,000, then 25p
- Mopeds and motorbikes: 24p flat
- Bicycles: actual costs (parts, repairs, depreciation)

One thing most drivers miss: every mile counts from the moment you go online to the moment you log off, not just the miles with an order on. The empty leg back to a busier zone, the loop after a Multi-Collect, the wait at the restaurant. Just Eat doesn't pay for those. HMRC does.

A 20,000-mile year on a moped = £4,800 off your taxable income. £960 in tax if you are basic rate. Most couriers don't track it because the platform apps don't show mileage at all.

The HMRC Digital Platform Reporting rules quietly went live on 1 January 2024. The first batch of reports landed at HMRC by 31 January 2026, covering the full 2025 calendar year.

That means every UK gig platform - Just Eat, Uber Eats, Deliveroo, Stuart, Amazon Flex, DPD, Evri - has now sent HMRC the annual earnings of every self-employed driver and courier on their books, broken down per worker.

It is the biggest UK gig-work compliance shift since the Uber Supreme Court ruling, and most drivers I speak to do not know it has happened. The CIOT and LITRG have been writing about this all year. The awareness gap among the people it actually affects is enormous.

Two effects worth flagging.

For drivers who file Self Assessment honestly, nothing changes. The platform reports just back up what they already declared.
