=== START ===
Tax tip of the week

Capital allowances vs simplified expenses - once you pick one for a vehicle, you can't switch later for that same vehicle.

Simplified expenses (the 55p/25p AMAP rate) is what 90% of gig drivers should use. It includes fuel, insurance, servicing, depreciation - no receipts needed.

Capital allowances (claim a % of the vehicle's purchase price + actual running costs) only beats AMAP if you've got a high-value electric vehicle and low business mileage. Worth running both numbers before deciding.

If you drive your own car for work AND your employer pays you per-mile mileage, but at a rate LOWER than HMRC's 55p, you can claim the gap back from HMRC.

- Employer paid you: £1,250
- HMRC's rate would have paid you: £2,750
- Gap of £1,500 - HMRC refunds you tax on that
- At basic rate: £300 back. At higher rate: £600 back

=== START ===
The most common question we get from new self-employed drivers:

What actually counts as a business mile?

Quick guide:

YES (claim it):
- Home to first pickup of the shift (yes, even from your driveway)
- Between jobs / customers / drops
- To and from a depot
- To a one-off training course
- To a temporary workplace

NO (don't claim it):
- Daily commute to a fixed office or workshop
- Personal trips (school run, shopping)
- The driving lessons you took on a Tuesday evening

GREY AREA (defensible if you justify):
- A school run that becomes a delivery (split the journey)
- Lunch out with a client (case-by-case)
