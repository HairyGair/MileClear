## 1) Long form — MileClear Facebook group

> 🚀 **MileClear 1.2.1 is live on the App Store**
>
> Apple finally cleared the build this morning. If you have the app already, head to the App Store and tap Update — you'll see the new stuff on next launch. If you don't, search MileClear and grab it free.
>
> Here's what changed:
>
> **HMRC raised the mileage rate to 55p.** From the 2026-27 tax year (any trip on or after 6 April 2026), cars and vans claim 55p per mile for the first 10,000 business miles — up from the old 45p. 25p above 10k and 24p for motorbikes are unchanged. MileClear picks the right rate per trip date automatically, so older trips stay at 45p and newer ones get 55p, with no fiddling needed. The driver doing 18,800 business miles a year is now £1,000 better off at tax time.
>
> **Expenses, finally a first-class section.** Avatar menu → Expenses. Every taxable purchase — parking, tolls, ULEZ, phone bill, repairs, accountant fees — in one place, categorised by the 17 SA103S boxes HMRC actually uses on Self Assessment. Snap any receipt and Apple Vision OCR pulls the amount, date and vendor straight off the paper. On-device only — nothing leaves your phone.
>
> **Bank-feed Inbox (Pro).** Connect your bank with Open Banking and every transaction lands in a triage screen. One tap to Accept as Earning, Accept as Expense, or Ignore. The categoriser learns from every override — mark "Costa Coffee" as Subsistence twice, the third time it's pre-selected with high confidence.
>
> **Project / client labels** for freelancers with multiple income streams — tag trips, earnings and expenses with a project name, and the per-project P&L view tells you which client actually paid after fuel + parking + expenses came out.
>
> **The Help & Tutorials section got a full rebuild** — 8 sections, ~69 topics. Pretty much every feature now has a one-tap explainer. Tap the avatar → Help & Tutorials.
>
> Three asks while I have you:
> 1. If you've been using MileClear and not yet rated it, a fresh 5★ review on the App Store genuinely moves the needle for a small indie like us. 🙏
> 2. Bugs, weirdness, feature requests — drop them in the comments here or DM me. I read everything personally.
> 3. The unlimited free tracking is still the headline — most apps cap you at 40 drives a month. Tell a driver mate who'd benefit. The more of us there are, the better the benchmark numbers get for everyone.
>
> Cheers for being part of this from the early days. More to come. — Anthony
